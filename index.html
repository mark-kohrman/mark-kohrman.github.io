What I'm About

Chicago based web developer who loves to solve problems and build things; especially with software! I've always had an interest in tech, but was inspired to take coding seriously as a career through my love of reading. A book about the Effective Altruism community suggested that software engineering is a career through which I can make a positive difference in the world, which is what I'm all about. In my free time, I enjoy distance running/biking, playing sports (real and fantasy), listening to nerdy podcasts about tech and economics, and volunteering at my local dog shelter.

Software Developer

My career in tech started as a Full Stack PHP Developer at Dealer eProcess and I have never been more passionate about what I do. We build car websites for auto dealers and we do it well. I also enjoy working on personal projects. Growing up, I wanted to be a linebacker in the NFL. After weighing in at under 100 pounds my freshman year of high school, I started to think I have the body frame of a fantasy football player. My love of football led me build a fantasy football app called "Fanalyze" for my capstone project at Actualize Coding Bootcamp. My app allows the fantasy football player to create teams and analyze whether they want to start or sit players based on weather projections the app provides. I used Ruby on Rails for the backend, Vue.js for the frontend and two different APIs for live sports and weather data. My latest deployment is a movie app that allows the user to search and rate their favorite movies. I'm currently working on a meditation app that allows the user to find places to meet with other users in the app and will have a video conferencing element (Rails for the backend and React for the frontend). Scroll down to my portfolio to see all of my projects and my Github .
