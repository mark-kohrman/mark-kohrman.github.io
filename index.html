What I'm About

Chicago based web developer who loves to solve problems and build things; especially with software! I've always had an interest in tech, but was inspired to take coding seriously as a career through my love of reading. A book about the Effective Altruism community suggested that software engineering is a career thorough which I can make a positive difference in the world, which is what I'm all about. In my free time, I enjoy distance running/biking, playing sports (real and fantasy), listening to nerdy podcasts about tech and economics, and volunteering at my local dog shelter.

Software Engineer

Growing up I wanted to be a linebacker in the NFL. After weighing in at under 100 pounds my freshmen year of high school, I started to think I have the frame of a coach or manager. My love of football led me build a fantasy football app called "Fanalyze". My app allows the fantasy football manager to create teams and analyze whether they want to start or sit players based on weather projections the app provides. I used Ruby on Rails for the backend, Vue.js for the frontend and two different APIs for live sports and weather data. I'm working on rebuilding the app with a free sports API using Python and Django for the backend so I can take it live via Heroku (view the demo in my link below)

Bookworm

I love to write code and discovered this through my love of reading books. William MacAskill's "Doing Good Better" introduced me to Effective Altruism and how evidence and reason can be used to make the world a better place. Biographies of historical figures have also piqued my literary interest from Benjamin Franklin to Steve Jobs (Walter Isaacson writes a mean bio). Angela Duckworth's book "Grit" taught me the importance of perserverance. Being a tech nerd, I was interested to hear that J.C.R Licklider is the true visionary of the personal computer in Mitchell Waldrop's "The Dream Machine". But this is only the beginning of the list.
